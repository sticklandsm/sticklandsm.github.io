Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

There was a situation where I was trying to help a client move the companies finance database from their current computer, to their new computer. It was literally on a single computer, not on a server, or the cloud. The problem was I didn't know anything about this software/database. I had to figure out how to use the software first of all in order to be able to work out how to export the database somewhere else. We were told that if a problem takes longer than an hour we should escalate it to someone with more experience. This rule doesn't work in practice with problems like this because no one knows how this software works. This caused tension with my team because I had to spend so long on this one project. After many, many hours I just ended up copying and pasting the whole application and the database over to the shared file server, then onto their new computer. It all seemed to be working so I signed off for the day. A month later the director of the company called us, furious, because I had accidentally left a copy of the database in the IT folder on the shared sever which in theory anyone could access. Even though the database was password protected when going through the software, the actual files could just be opened in Excel if you dug into the folder far enough. Bad program. Do not recommend. Still my fault though.

This was an ethical question because at the very start I could have told the client that we're not going to help with that part. I would have been in the right, and I would also gain a lot of spare time by not doing it. But I knew that it would be a nightmare for this person to try to contact this massive finance company and try to explain what their issue was. They would probably give up and go back to their old computer, which will eventually fail, and lead to bigger problems like people not getting paid on time. I still don't know if I made the right decision to help.

 

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.


I have severe ADHD-I (I = Inattentive type) that was only recently diagnosed In 2020. I've been a C student my whole life. Studying, and homework, and assignments were very difficult but I got through based on what I absorbed in the classroom. There's a common pattern where adults will put special interest into kids with ADHD because it seems like if they just put in more effort, did the homework, the assignments, studied more, then they would be top of the class. When this doesn't work out, the kid is accused of being lazy, unmotivated, disobedient etc. This pattern can occur over and over well into adulthood. Eventually that kid will start believing those descriptors.

Here's a fun twitter thread which goes over that pattern in more detail:

https://twitter.com/the_weed/status/1534592771792572418

Despite me being diagnosed, and medicated now, that pattern has shaped who I am and has heavily affected my values.
